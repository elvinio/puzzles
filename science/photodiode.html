Photodiode

// how light becomes current

01

Photon strikes the depletion region. A photon carries energy E = hf. If that energy exceeds the semiconductor's bandgap, it kicks an electron free — creating an electron–hole pair.

02

The built-in electric field separates the pair instantly. The depletion region's field sweeps the electron (−) toward the P-side and the hole (+) toward the N-side — before they can recombine.

03

Charge flows through the external circuit. The separated carriers are collected at the metal contacts, driving a photocurrent through the wire. More light → more pairs → stronger current.
